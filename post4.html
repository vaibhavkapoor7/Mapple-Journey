What I wish I knew before moving here

Moving to Canada is a big step, full of dreams and goals, and sometimes surprises. That's why today we want to share with you how things can be very different from what we imagined: the culture, the climate, the job search, and even daily routines.

By Maple Journey Team • Updated Nov 2025 • 3 min read

  • Tips
  • Newcomer
  • Canada

1. It takes time to feel at home

The first few days can feel a little lonely, but over time, things gradually improve and become routine.

2. Making connections is more important than you think

In Canada, the people you meet are often as important as your knowledge. For this reason, it's very important to network and attend events where you can meet many people and make friends.

Image by geralt from Pixabay — “Travel to Canada.”

3. The weather isn't just "cold," it's a way of life

Canada is a very cold country, which is why it's not just a certain time of year that's cold, but practically all year round. That's why it's recommended to bring warm clothes so you don't have a bad time.

“Image courtesy of Destination Canada.”

4. Canadian friendliness is real… but sometimes it can seem distant

Friendliness in Canada isn't just from a few people; it's Canadian culture itself that fosters a friendly environment, but building deep friendships takes time. Keep trying; it's worth it.

5. Your accent is your story

Canada is a country of immigrants, so don't be afraid to make mistakes; we all go through it.
