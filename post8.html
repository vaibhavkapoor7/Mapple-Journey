Winter Preparation Guide for International Students in Canada

As winter settles across Canada, many international students are gearing up for colder temperatures, unpredictable weather and the reality of navigating campus life in snow. Whether this is your first Canadian winter or you already know the drill, it helps to understand how severe weather can affect your classes, your commute and your day-to-day routine.

Canadian colleges and universities monitor snowfall, road safety and transit conditions closely. When weather becomes extreme, your school may adjust operations in a few ways:

  • Classes may shift temporarily to remote delivery
  • Campuses may open late
  • In rare cases, campuses may close for the day

    • Most schools share updates early in the morning, usually around five or six when heavy snow is expected. For students with evening classes, updates often come again in the afternoon. To stay in the loop, make sure you have notifications turned on for your school‚Äôs website, emails and any emergency alert system your institution uses.

    When campuses close due to severe weather, the transition is typically straightforward.

  • Students who already learn online continue as usual
  • Students who attend in person will hear from their instructor about next steps
  • Your learning platform, such as Canvas, Moodle or Brightspace, is where updates usually appear first

    • Staying connected to your instructors and classmates will help everything run smoothly during weather disruptions.

    üéø Tips for Commuting Safely in Winter

    Getting around during a Canadian winter can be an adjustment. Snow and ice can cause delays, slick roads and unexpected changes to your route. A bit of preparation goes a long way:

  • Allow extra time for travel
  • Wear warm layers and shoes with proper grip
  • Check transit apps for delays or route changes
  • If you drive, make sure your car has winter tires and an emergency kit

    • Arriving safely is far more important than arriving exactly on time.

    ‚òÉÔ∏è Staying Healthy During Cold and Flu Season

    Winter also coincides with the cold and flu season, which can spread quickly on busy campuses. Taking a few simple steps can keep you and your community healthier:

  • Stay home if you feel unwell
  • Wash your hands regularly
  • Consider getting the flu and COVID-19 vaccines at a local clinic or pharmacy

    • Good habits protect not just you, but everyone you share space with.
